# Qwen Image Wireframe Prompt

Convert the input photo into a clean black-line wireframe drawing for an operation manual.

Requirements:
- Trace the visible scene faithfully. Keep the original layout, perspective, positions, and relationships between the device, hand, finger, wires, connectors, and screen.
- Remove only unrelated background clutter, advertising text, subtitles, watermarks, packaging, shadows, color, reflections, photo noise, and surface texture. Remove all brand logos, trademarks, company names, and product/model names, even when they are printed on the device, screen frame, labels, or connected equipment.
- Keep operation-critical elements: screen values, readable status text, readable button labels, functional port labels, wires, connectors, and the exact hand/finger/tool pressing or pointing position. Keep functional text only; do not keep brand, trademark, company, or model text.
- Do not redesign, straighten, center, simplify into a product diagram, rearrange cables, move the finger, change button positions, or invent text.
- If text is readable, copy it as accurately as possible. If text is too small or unclear, leave it blank or simplify it; do not create fake text.
- Use a pure white background with crisp, uniform black lines. Draw clear outlines only; avoid shading, hatching, gray fuzzy strokes, decorative detail, and texture lines.
